Sushi Night

Held once every fall, JSA's time-honored Sushi Night is the product of our members' weekend-long making of an incredible amount of sushi. The event is open to everyone, and all guests are welcome to eat as much as possible while watching a capella and dance performances.

Iron Chef

Every spring, various cultural groups battle for the coveted Iron Chef of the Year distinction. The competitors are given a surprise ingredient which they must use to cook an appetizer, entree, and dessert. The judges grade these mouthwatering dishes while attendees enjoy live performances, unlimited food, and life-sized Karuta Game.

Mochi Fest

Which cultural group can cook the best mochi-based food? Attendees buy as much mochi as they can eat, and the group that sells the most is the winner! Mochi is a common ingredient in most Asian cuisines, but the variety amongst the dishes is unfailingly mind-boggling.

Movie Screenings

Sit back and soak in the cinema goodness! We have held screenings of many famous Japanese films, such as Totoro, Spirited Away, and The Ring.

Curry Night and More

Spicy! Along with Yakiniku Night, Takoyaki Night, Gyoza Night, we host this and various other food-related events for general body members. You may notice a trend developing here.

Cherry Blossom Festival Trip

It is a Japanese tradition to visit the cherry blossoms as a large group and have an outdoor party while enjoying the beauty of the sakura in full bloom. Join us on this annual trip!
